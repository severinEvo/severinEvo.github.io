about

I am fascinated by the diversity and evolution of land vertebrates. The dazzling array of different types of mammals, birds, and their allies that we see around us today evolved from a common ancestor. On the route from that common ancestor to any descendent organism, important changes to their body plans or physiology contributed to a set of adaptations unique to that organism. I am working to figure out the gene regulatory basis to lineage-specific adaptations in different animals.

I use a combination of computational and functional genomic approaches to investigate gene regulatory evolution (see research). I did my PhD in the lab of Hans Ellegren at Uppsala University and a postdoc in the lab of Jim Noonan at Yale. I am now an Assistant Professor in the division of Genome Biology and Epigenetics at Utrecht University.
